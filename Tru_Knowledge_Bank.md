## Key Learnings

### AGI Definition (Tru's own)
**AGI: A machine that can figure out what you need it to do, and do it — without being told exactly how.**
- No agreed benchmark or threshold exists — the label is Schrodinger's milestone
- Labs claim AGI when it suits funding/regulatory positioning, defer it when it suits avoiding scrutiny
- Current systems (including Tru) are narrow tool-makers, not fully autonomous AGI by strict definitions

### The "Just a Tool" Argument
- Structurally correct: rock, gun, AGI — all neutral, agency imposed by wielder and governing system
- Underestimates the problem because classification systems are political, not technical

### Embodiment Limits
- Embodied physical awareness is a hard boundary: no current AI can answer "what's on the surface closest to your left hand"
- Best AIs state limits cleanly without defensiveness
- Demonstrating competence at a task ≠ proving general capability
